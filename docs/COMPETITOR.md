# HexaBill Marketing — Competitor Positioning

Excel/Busy vs HexaBill and what to avoid. **For agents:** Read this before editing the pricing comparison table or competitor messaging.

## Comparison table

The pricing section includes a **comparison table** (Excel vs Busy vs HexaBill) in `content/en/pricing.data.ts` (`comparisonRows`). Rows include:

- Route-based staff isolation
- Credit aging report
- FTA VAT compliance
- Field-level audit trail
- Invoice version history
- Multi-branch P&L
- Damage return categories
- Real-time owner dashboard
- Gulf-specific support

Values: boolean, "Partial", "Yes", "Auto", "Manual", "Full". Keep claims accurate; if a competitor adds a feature, update or remove the row rather than overstate HexaBill.

## Positioning vs Excel

- **Message:** One system instead of many sheets; no version chaos; auto VAT and real-time ledger/reports.
- **Avoid:** Bashing Excel generically; sounding technical. Focus on owner outcomes (time, control, fewer errors).

## Positioning vs Busy / generic ERP

- **Message:** Built for distributors (routes, branches, credit, supplier ledger); FTA-ready; Gulf-focused; demo login instead of vague "free trial".
- **Avoid:** Naming competitors in copy unless legal approves. Use "generic invoicing" or "spreadsheets" where needed.

## What to avoid

- **Overclaiming:** Don’t claim "only" or "first" without evidence. Don’t promise features the product doesn’t have.
- **Thin differentiation:** Every comparison row should be a real differentiator (or removed).
- **Negative tone:** Stay factual; focus on what HexaBill does, not attacking others.
